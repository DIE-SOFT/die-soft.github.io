## Pre-Alpha Demo for Content Creators üíæ

I want to give a special shout-out to the Sleepwalkers and Guardians that have taken advantage of their ‚Äúüíæ Early Builds Access‚Äù reward and provided feedback on the state of things. Maybe it‚Äôs just some encouragement, maybe it‚Äôs sharing with me a bug you spotted, or even some suggestions for improvements. Y‚Äôall have really helped a lot to get things into a state that I was much happier sharing the build with select content creators to help get the word out about our Steam page launch. These content creators were playing this same build y‚Äôall helped polish and it seems to have been very well received both by the streamers and their viewers.

Many of you probably recall that we did something similar with a build for the Kickstarter launch, so I thought I‚Äôd go over some of the major things that changed since that build to give you an idea of what's new:

- The biggest major change is that this demo build has moved from using a baked-in Unity scene (simply loading in all of the content that was in the demo all at once), and is now using the Slumberland JSON data that I‚Äôm authoring in NemoMaker and allows a vast, seamless world that loads in around the player as they move through it. This doesn‚Äôt make for a big, noticeable change for the players of the demo, but was a requirement to grow into the scale we need for the full game. I‚Äôm really happy we managed this with only one major bug popping up because of it.

There has been tons of polish, so I‚Äôll just rattle off a few of my favs:

- Not only can you now pet the cat, but you can also pet the boss! This isn‚Äôt something I had even anticipated, but it‚Äôs one of the nice things that happens when you create a small feature and find new/fun ways to use it.
- Nemo‚Äôs ducking, spinning, and hand-over-hand movement while dangling are all new features which have little-or-no functional impact but just help sell the animations of the world.
- I think most or all of the SFX in the old build were pulled from a sound library, so most SFX in the current build are drastically improved thanks to Jonathan Baken working on them.
- Huge improvements to the Rocktopus fight (Oblivion eyes, difficulty tweaks, tentacles retracting, hit pause, visual hit effect, etc. etc.)

Major features are now accessible:

- The map is fully functioning (though missing some of the icons that will appear within the map). You‚Äôve got your *standard Metroidvania map* which is a grid of screen-sized chunks forming rooms which are added to the map as you get to them, and filled in as you visit each chunk.
- Both Pajamas and Little Buddies are now working. There are still lots of PJs and Buddies to make, but the features are working as intended and you can find one Little Buddy and three PJs in the current build.
- The status page is fully functioning. You can see which Toys/PJs/Little Buddies you have available and get notes about how they work, as well as see Notes on Nemo‚Äôs standard abilities and the Health/Moon Meter/Candy HUD.
- You can now rescue your first Guardian and dispel Oblivion from the Dreamswept Plains. This will be a recurring behavior in most zones, so I‚Äôm super excited to show how this works as part of the demo/introduction to the game.
- The serialization to disk is much better as well, and despite a minor bug when walking keys into doors instead of throwing them, everything has been auto-saving as expected with no hiccups that I‚Äôve spotted.

Lots of new content:

- The Gumdrop Gardens tileset is in a really good place now.
- This is the first time everyone is seeing the finalized Mushroom Marsh biome.
- New tracks for the different zones, the bedroom, the title screen, and the Rocktopus fight.
- A larger portion of Dreamswept Plains to explore, and just generally a better sell on the idea of how exploration/shortcuts/back-tracking will tend to be a part of the gameplay.
  

So while there are still some features either missing or in need of some polish, the heart of the gameplay mechanics are in there, so it‚Äôs quite a change from that early build where it was me trying my best to approximate what it might look like in the future by *hinting at* features.

One of the things I know I‚Äôm going to want in the Mushroom Marsh, is some bouncy mushrooms. Bouncy platforms are such a staple for platformers, the trick is getting them just right. Here‚Äôs what I‚Äôm testing out now:

![img](https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/039/781/560/2ff0b26ce3bc5d9989d8b0f7dd70549d_original.gif?ixlib=rb-4.0.2&w=700&fit=max&v=1674855902&gif-q=50&q=92&s=d871b37e6514a371be1bdafe24502982)

While my physics system has restitution, and we could simply just simply make a platform fully bouncy that way, that approach is too *realistic* and isn‚Äôt predictable for the player (bounce height is determined by your incoming velocity). So instead this platform will just bounce whatever hits it up to a target height. If that thing can jump or pogo (eg. the player) doing so will cause the bounce to be even higher.

You can see here, it‚Äôs not very realistic that as soon as the player steps over the bouncy platform, they bounce up, but it‚Äôs certainly more fun I think.

And here‚Äôs another idea, this one intended for the Gumdrop Gardens and inspired by Donkey Kong Country 2‚Äôs honey. These sticky blocks will immobilize anyone touching them and you‚Äôll need to press jump or down + jump to jump or release from it. This is a fun way to introduce a player to the idea of wall climbing before giving them the Monster Claw.

![img](https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/039/781/564/a4cfe6dc065f4195214cb7d8e4b6b5da_original.gif?ixlib=rb-4.0.2&w=700&fit=max&v=1674855936&gif-q=50&q=92&s=fc25d5b04bd1718af610ab7d162551bb)

I‚Äôm fairly sure it will be fun sticking to and jumping from the sides, but having this allows me to explore whether or not they‚Äôd be fun as a floor surface to potentially get stuck on and require hopping to advance along it.

I‚Äôve also been exploring new enemy behaviors. The classic Metroidvania style enemy is of course Metroid‚Äôs Geemer, which simply follows along a surface.

![img](https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/039/781/567/f3cdaea4c6d2fa90b43163e7cab9fc59_original.gif?ixlib=rb-4.0.2&w=700&fit=max&v=1674855977&gif-q=50&q=92&s=9e4d91597e6d63b4c1c085d4cb22967c)

And here‚Äôs something we haven‚Äôt seen in the game yet, but I‚Äôve been excited to get in here to try out: projectile-firing enemies. This one is a very simple design inspired by Mario‚Äôs Bullet Bills.

![img](https://ksr-ugc.imgix.net/assets/039/781/581/fe0c2ece470909358b58b2fa0d4b5362_original.gif?ixlib=rb-4.0.2&w=700&fit=max&v=1674856091&gif-q=50&q=92&s=a77b79d7eac606ee5f6b36d55be826ea)
